Vertical Precision

Through experiments using laser power curve tests, it was determined that the smallest stable precision achievable is a cut depth of 1.5mm. When the height difference between adjacent areas is less than approximately 1.5mm, the expansion and deformation of the material begin to couple, causing the step boundaries to become blurred and making it difficult to stabilize the height difference. When the height difference exceeds 1.5mm, the layer structures can form clear and stable height differences, ensuring precision and reliability in the final result.

Vertical Precision Test

Process

  1. Prepare the Material: Choose a material with a consistent thickness and texture.
  2. Set Cutting Parameters: Adjust the laser cutter parameters for depth control based on the target material's behavior.
  3. Perform Vertical Tests: Test different height differences between layers, observing the stability and clarity of the height differences.
  4. Adjust and Finalize Settings: Fine-tune the cutting parameters to ensure that a height difference of 1.5mm or more is maintained.
  5. Final Observation: Observe the cut material to verify that the step boundaries are clearly defined and stable.
