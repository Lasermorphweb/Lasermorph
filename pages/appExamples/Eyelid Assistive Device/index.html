Enhanced Sponge Uses

This collection focuses on application directions that extend the original capabilities of cellulose sponge products. Instead of treating sponge as a flat, disposable cleaning material, these cases use depth-programmed swelling, double-sided engraving, and joining strategies to create richer geometry, more specific functions, and stronger product identity.

Dual-material Sponge Block

By engraving rough zigzag surfaces with different geometric density on the left and right sides of the same compressed sponge block, this case creates differentiated friction and hardness after activation. The two sides can therefore serve different cleaning targets, such as gentle wiping for delicate surfaces on one side and stronger scrubbing for stubborn dirt on the other. The case demonstrates how digital fabrication can integrate multiple cleaning behaviors into a single sponge object.

Size: 100 x 60 x 15 mm. Time: 16 min.

3D Bath Sponge

This case uses double-sided laser engraving and alignment to write volumetric features into both sides of a compressed cellulose sponge sheet. After water activation, the flat sheet expands into a three-dimensional sponge, such as a bear-shaped bath object, extending conventional sponge products from 2D outlines to expressive 3D forms. The result supports more emotional and personalized product design while preserving the basic cleaning function.

Bear: 100 x 100 x 50 mm. Time: 4 h 20 min.

Children's Bath Blocks

Based on mortise-and-tenon structures and double-sided laser engraving, this concept turns compressed sponge sheets into stackable and connectable volumetric bath blocks. After water activation, the blocks become larger and softer, creating a safer gripping and assembling experience for children. The material itself also has cleaning functionality, allowing play and bathing to happen at the same time.

Marathon Cooling Sponge

This case uses the strong water-retention ability and compact dry-state volume of cellulose sponge to improve the transport, storage, and distribution efficiency of cooling sponges for marathon events. With double-sided engraving, the object can take on volumetric mascot forms such as a Qingyuan chicken rather than remaining a flat cutout. The result combines practical cooling performance with local cultural communication and supports future customization for different events.

Qingyuan Chicken: 67 x 82 x 30 mm. Time: 66 min per side.

Mortise-and-tenon Storage Box

This case turns compressed sponge sheets into self-locking two-part storage boxes. The edge arrays use small mortise-and-tenon connectors, while the central areas are engraved with recessed cavities that fit target objects. After water activation, the tenons lock without extra glue or fasteners and the swollen sponge forms a soft protective interior. After use, the box can be hot-pressed back to a compact state for repeated storage and transport.

Edge tenon test: for 15 mm thick sponge, a 7.5 mm high edge tenon with 5 mm diameter cylindrical connectors and 2 mm spacing provided the most stable bite.

Box-s: 65 x 65 x 30 mm, edge width 16 mm, inner space 33 x 33 x 10 mm. Objects: SD cards, TF cards, rings, earrings, coins, micro USB drives, AirTags.

Box-m: 142 x 142 x 65 mm, edge width 16 mm, inner space 110 x 110 x 40 mm. Objects: watches, bracelets, sunglasses, mobile drives, recorders, passports, small badges.

Box-l: 275 x 275 x 95 mm, edge width 16 mm, inner space 243 x 243 x 70 mm. Objects: iPad, Kindle, drawing tablet, compact camera, vinyl records, folding keyboards.

Tactile Memory Postcard

This case compresses a souvenir object back into postcard format. Laser engraving creates tactile topography in the dry sheet, and water activation unfolds the postcard into a relief-like 3D object. Future versions may combine hydrophobic masking or watercolor activation so that swelling also reveals hidden color or staged visual interaction.

Moon Surface: 148 x 100 x 30 mm. Time: 2 h 33 min / 2 h 36 min.

Mouse Wrist Rest

This prototype studies how wrist-support geometry influences hand posture. The design focuses on height transition, support curvature, and the front slope rather than fine surface detail, so a 2D gradient-depth pattern is enough to generate a convincing support volume after swelling. The case shows how the method can support fast product prototyping with real volume and soft tactile feedback.

Size: 158 x 89 x 28 mm. Time: 3 h 29 min.
