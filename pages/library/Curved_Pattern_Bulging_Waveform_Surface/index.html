Bulging Waveform Surface

How It Works

The Bulging Waveform Surface is created by densely arranging interlocking Bulging Wave Line 2 curve cuts across a surface. Upon inflation, the structure bends into a continuous three-dimensional form. This geometry directs the actuator to deform along the orientation of the embedded air channels, enabling controlled and directional bending.

Standard Sample Parameters

  • Material Thickness: 0.07 mm per side
  • Scale: 60.0 mm × 80.0 mm
  • Channel Width at Narrowest Point: 3.3 mm
  • Number of Cycles: 3
  • Number of Rows: 4

Customization Options and Actuation Control

KiriInflate structures offer high programmability of geometric and mechanical properties through customization of slit patterns on 2D surfaces, allowing for rapid formation of stretchable 3D geometries with tailored mechanical behaviors. Key parameters to adjust include:

Scale

As the scale increases, the curvature remains the same at first, and then decreases. Larger inflatables generally increase stretchable length and contraction range, while both contraction and tensile forces increase. However, increasing sizes may lead to a slight decrease in the contraction ratio due to material resistance. Here we assume that the material resistance plays a dominant role.

Key Structure Parameter: Width at the Narrowest Point & Number of Rows

As the width at the narrowest point increases, the contraction ratio first increases and then decreases, while the curvature decreases slightly but remains relatively stable. As the number of rows increases, the curvature increases; whereas a higher number of cycles leads to a decrease in curvature.

Material Thickness

As the thickness increases, the curvature remains relatively unchanged, while the contraction ratio gradually decreases. In this case, the thickness mainly limits the contraction ratio, but has little effect on the bending curvature.
