Game Console

This case investigates the use of lasermorph for larger handheld or tabletop entertainment hardware. Compared with a simple shell study, the console prototype places more emphasis on overall massing, interaction zones, and the balance between soft tactile contact and volumetric presence. It is useful for evaluating how a device reads as both an object to hold and an object to operate, especially when button placement, body thickness, and edge transitions all influence use.

The case demonstrates that the system can support expressive prototype bodies for interactive devices that must be both visually legible and physically approachable. In entertainment hardware, form is rarely neutral: it communicates playfulness, comfort, and intended mode of engagement. By enabling rapid iteration of these properties in a materially soft prototype, lasermorph provides a productive bridge between speculative industrial design and embodied interaction testing.
