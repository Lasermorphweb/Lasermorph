Ergonomic Wrist Rest

This case is a desktop ergonomic prototype used to evaluate wrist support, height transition, and front-edge slope through quickly formed continuous curvature. Because the goal is early-stage validation rather than high-fidelity finishing, lasermorph is well suited to testing posture-related geometry with low time cost. It allows the designer to quickly move from section-based digital reasoning to a physically touchable support object that can be judged under actual desk conditions.

The case shows how soft macro-curvature can be rapidly embodied from 2D coding and then physically assessed through direct use. This matters because ergonomic judgment often depends on bodily contact, gradual pressure distribution, and the feel of transition zones, all of which are difficult to infer from digital rendering alone. By providing a real object with thickness, resilience, and recoverability, the method yields richer feedback during the early phase of design.
