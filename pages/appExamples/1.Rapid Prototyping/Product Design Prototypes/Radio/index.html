Radio

This case examines compact radio form factors as a study in front-facing interface composition and enclosure proportioning. The prototype allows designers to quickly evaluate the relationship between speaker openings, control areas, and the body outline without relying on fully engineered assemblies. It is especially effective for studying "face logic," where the distribution of interface elements has to appear balanced, intuitive, and visually coherent even before detailed mechanical integration is considered.

The case is also useful for comparing retro-inspired and contemporary proportions in a materially soft, rapidly produced format. Radios carry strong typological memory, so even small changes in aspect ratio, thickness, and frontal organization can shift the object's character substantially. As a result, this case shows how lasermorph can be used not just to test physical form, but to test product identity and historical reference through quickly embodied mock-ups.
