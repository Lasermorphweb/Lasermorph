Rain-Triggered Urban Interfaces

This case develops context-aware public signage that remains visually quiet in dry conditions and becomes legible only when activated by rain or water. The project uses the material's rapid volumetric response to link environmental change directly to information display, allowing warnings such as slippery surfaces or flood risk to emerge without electricity. The signage therefore does not simply exist in the environment; it synchronizes its visibility with environmental relevance.

The case demonstrates how zero-energy actuation can enrich urban micro-infrastructure while aligning visibility with real situational need. Because the message only appears when the triggering condition exists, the interface reduces constant visual noise while increasing contextual precision. This makes the project especially suitable for parks, riverbanks, slopes, fitness zones, and other public micro-environments where static warnings are often ignored but rain-related risk is highly variable and time-sensitive.
