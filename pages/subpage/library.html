Shapes

Level 0: Surface Slopes

Creating angled surfaces with adjustable slope angles. Units arranged in patterns form unique geometries with smooth transitions between different angles.

Level 1: Single Bends

Creating shapes through surface rotation with single curvature control. Shape generated by rotating a surface 360 degrees around an axis, forming a rotational body.

Level 2: Complex Curves

Introduces composite curvature controlling both positive and negative curvature within the same surface. Enables formation of negative Gaussian curvature surfaces.

Level 3: Volumetric Shapes

Creating complex 3D structures beyond simple shapes. Users build models in design software and import into LaserMorph for processing.

Scalability

Single-sided

The most basic technique where design is cut on one side only. Suitable for simpler designs and provides a straightforward approach to creating 3D shapes.

Double-sided

Cutting both front and back faces. Model split into two parts, each side processed separately. Suitable for creating complex 3D shapes with detail on both sides.

Side-plane

Cutting along side surfaces of the material. Requires adjustments in laser parameters for vertical direction, ensuring precise cutting with minimal horizontal deformation.

Six-sided

Combines single-sided, double-sided, and side-plane techniques to cut all six faces. Perfect for creating fully enclosed 3D shapes by processing each side separately.

Horizontal Joining

Creating interlocking zigzag edges that allow sections to be joined horizontally. Design relies on puzzle-like interlocking patterns for larger expanded shapes.

Vertical Joining

Modular interlocking pieces with protruding and recessed features along vertical surfaces. Enables assembly of larger structures by interlocking parts vertically.

Resolution

Vertical Precision

Smallest stable precision: 1.5mm cut depth. Height differences exceeding 1.5mm form clear, stable layer structures. Below 1.5mm, expansion causes coupled deformation.

Surface Quality

Cross-path scanning strategy (adjacent paths perpendicular) significantly reduces striped structure compared to single-direction scanning, producing smoother surfaces.

Structural Limits

Safe wall thickness threshold: 0.8mm. Wall thickness at or above 0.8mm ensures reliable manufacturing and structural stability after moisture-induced expansion.

Typography

Minimum text size: 1.0cm ideal for display, 0.75cm recognizable. Text smaller than 0.75cm becomes unreadable through the laser cutting process.

Morphing

Morphing

Four triggering factors control material lifecycle: Water Absorption (10x vertical expansion), Sealing (maintains activated state), Dehydration (locks shape), Compression (120C resets to flat).
