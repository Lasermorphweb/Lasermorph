Art and Expression

This collection focuses on cases where cellulose sponge is used as a medium for composition, visual experimentation, and tangible creative expression. Rather than only serving as a functional material, the sponge becomes a rapidly editable carrier for poster systems, floating installations, and symbolic form studies.

Physical Poster Design

Reference

Rapid Creative Poster Prototype

Based on double-sided laser engraving and mortise-and-tenon structures, this case develops a freely reconfigurable poster prototyping system. The system includes a preset baseboard and a series of detachable modules. Each module carries a small tenon at its bottom and can be inserted into a perforated grid-like array on the board. This allows users to rapidly test layouts, replace graphic elements, and create tangible poster compositions without relying on professional design software.

Size: baseboard 148 x 210 x 15 mm (A5 plane), each mortise unit 5 x 5 x 5 mm. Time: 1 h 47 min and follow-up iterations.

Notes: the black letters in the drawing are engraved into the baseboard, while the outlined rectangle marks the free-combination mortise array.

Water Tank Rapid Design Prototype

Reference

This direction combines a large flat poster-like base with an upper water tank and floating absorbent elements. It explores how the material can be used to prototype layered, water-activated compositions in which separate components swell, float, and rearrange inside a displayed environment.

Logic Symbol Elements

Reference

This ongoing direction studies how geometric or semantic symbols can be translated into tactile, swellable elements. The goal is to build a reusable vocabulary of physical symbols that can support communication, exhibition graphics, and composition studies in a material rather than purely screen-based form.
