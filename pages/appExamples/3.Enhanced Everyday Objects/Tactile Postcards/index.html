Tactile Postcard

This case explores the "dimensional reduction" of 3D souvenirs into flat, mailable artifacts. A compressed postcard stores topographic or symbolic relief information that is only fully revealed after hydration, turning shipping efficiency into part of the user experience. Instead of transporting a fully volumetric souvenir, the design delivers latent form that unfolds through a simple ritual, making activation part of the object's narrative and emotional value.

The case is especially effective for tourism, education, and commemorative design because it combines portability, delayed reveal, tactile learning, and ritualized activation in one artifact. The postcard becomes more than a carrier of image; it becomes a participatory device through which the receiver completes the transformation. This gives the object a strong sense of intimacy and surprise while still keeping it inexpensive to ship and easy to distribute.
