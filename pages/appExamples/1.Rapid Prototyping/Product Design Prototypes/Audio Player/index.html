Audio Player

This case uses a compact audio player enclosure to test how lasermorph performs in hand-sized electronic product prototyping. The emphasis is on fast generation of body volume, surface transitions, and component placement rather than final engineering tolerances. In a product-development context, this means the prototype is most useful when the designer is still evaluating body proportion, interaction zoning, and front-face arrangement, and does not yet need a production-grade enclosure.

The audio-player typology is especially suitable for demonstrating the system's ability to produce clear, compact, consumer-oriented forms. Small differences in width, thickness, and edge treatment strongly affect how such devices are perceived in the hand, and lasermorph makes these comparisons accessible within a short cycle. The case therefore shows that the method is effective not only for expressive large forms, but also for concise, precise, handheld objects where bodily experience and visual balance matter equally.
