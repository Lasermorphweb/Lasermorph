Sustainable Cases

This collection focuses on ecological and logistics-oriented applications. Here the material is valued not only for its geometry but also for its biodegradability, compact dry-state storage, moisture retention, and ability to become useful again after its initial task is completed.

Hermit Crab Habitat Design

Using the volume change and soft structural behavior of cellulose sponge after water activation, this case proposes pre-programmed structures for eco-tanks, including terrain, plant-like elements, and lightweight shelter components. Because the material is soft, moisture-retaining, and light, it is especially suitable for micro-environment building and hiding structures in hermit crab habitats.

Artificial Seaweed and Shelter

Hermit crabs naturally prefer environments with rich structures such as seagrass or gravel areas that provide shade, humidity, and climbing surfaces. With programmable cellulose sponge, multiple slender leaf-like strips can be laser-cut and anchored to a stable base. Compared with conventional plastic aquarium plants, this approach offers three advantages: it helps retain humidity after wetting, its density and bending can be changed through fabrication parameters, and its soft geometry lowers collision risk. Shelter structures should remain open rather than fully enclosed, using arches or coral-like branches to provide temporary cover.

Meadow Base

A larger-scale application is a seagrass-carpet base placed on the bottom of the habitat. The base can be designed as a thin plate of about 18 x 10 x 1.5 cm, with broad cross-cutting on the top surface so that the processed texture naturally forms a dense grass-like field. This both creates a complex environment for hiding and moving through and also stores water to form a local humidity buffer zone.

Modular Climbing Facade

In addition to horizontal ground coverage, the vertical space of the eco-tank can be used through modular climbing surfaces that support hermit crabs' climbing instinct.

Planning

If these elements are integrated into a 25 x 15 x 15 cm habitat, three areas can be formed: an open activity zone, a meadow zone, and a sheltered zone. A meadow base of around 18 x 10 cm can be placed in one region, with two or three clusters of artificial seaweed near its edge and one or two light shelter structures on the opposite side. The remaining space stays open for movement. This layout demonstrates both complex form generation and the creation of an observable miniature ecological environment.

Space-saving Shaped Packaging

Using the material behavior of staying flat while dry and swelling into shape after wetting, this case develops biodegradable cushioning packages that can replace EPS foam for bottles, strawberries, and other fragile or irregular products.

Packaging Workflow

1. Flat storage and transport: before use, the laser-processed cellulose sponge is stacked as thin 2D sheets, eliminating the empty-box volume common in conventional protective packaging.

2. Water-triggered conformal wrapping: when packaging is needed, the object is placed on the sheet and lightly sprayed with water. The internal structure swells rapidly and rises to fit the object's outer contour.

3. Drying and shape locking: after the moisture evaporates, the material regains stiffness and forms a customized shell with both physical support and shock absorption.

Sustainability Value

  • High logistics efficiency by transporting a 2D sheet and using it as a 3D package only when needed.
  • Biodegradability, with no microplastic pollution after disposal.
  • Secondary use, since the dried shell can be re-softened in water and reused as a cleaning sponge or a plant moisture pad.

Typical Scenarios

  • Irregular glassware and wine bottles, where the swollen sponge can lock around narrow necks and wide bodies without custom molding.
  • Fragile produce, where gradient engraving creates a soft, highly conformal cradle and the slightly damp material can also help retain freshness during short transport.
