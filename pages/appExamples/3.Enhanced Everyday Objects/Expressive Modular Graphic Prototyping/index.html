Expressive Modular Graphic Prototyping

LaserMorph is used here as a medium for visual communication and tactile expression rather than only as a functional form-making process. These studies examine how transformable relief, modular assembly, and spatialized graphics can enrich posters, souvenirs, and symbolic objects. The resulting artifacts exist between graphic design, product prototyping, and exhibition media, making them especially useful in scenarios where an image is expected to generate attention through transformation rather than through static display alone.

What unifies this category is the shift from static representation to activated representation. Graphics are no longer only looked at; they are unfolded, hydrated, assembled, and touched. This introduces time, surprise, and participation into what would otherwise remain a flat communication surface. In this category, LaserMorph functions not only as a fabrication process, but also as a narrative medium that structures when and how information appears.
