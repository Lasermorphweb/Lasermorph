Rapid Prototypes

At the middle stage of product design, prototypes are often used to validate overall massing, proportion, and basic body-product interaction rather than complete internal structures or high-precision details. For this kind of model, the process can use fast 2D subtractive fabrication to encode a corresponding depth field, and then rely on water-triggered swelling to generate a 3D volume in a short time.

In practice, the 2D fabrication step often takes only minutes, while the subsequent water activation unfolds the form almost immediately. By contrast, producing a solid model of similar scale with conventional 3D printing usually requires many hours of layer-by-layer printing. This makes the process particularly useful during design exploration, where multiple volume studies need to be generated and iterated quickly for evaluating form, posture, ergonomics, and broad user interaction.
