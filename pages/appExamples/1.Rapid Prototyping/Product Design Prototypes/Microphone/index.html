Microphone

This case studies a microphone-like handheld object with a distinct head-and-handle geometry. The prototype is suitable for evaluating grasp, silhouette recognition, and transition between functional zones in a single soft volumetric body. A microphone archetype is especially useful because its formal logic is immediately recognizable, which makes it easier to judge whether the prototype communicates use, directionality, and handling through shape alone.

The case shows that lasermorph can quickly form recognizable product archetypes even when the object requires a strong difference between local diameters and a continuous tactile profile. It is therefore not only a study in form generation, but also a study in product readability. The resulting prototype can support early discussion of gesture, holding posture, and functional hierarchy before any detailed internal engineering is introduced.
