Six-sided

Six-sided carving combines the techniques of single-sided, double-sided, and side-plane carving to cut all six faces of a material. This approach is perfect for creating fully enclosed 3D shapes by processing each side separately. It involves cutting the top and bottom faces first, followed by the four side faces, and ensures that all parts of the model are carved.

Six-sided Carving

Process

  1. Slice the Model into Six Faces: Start by slicing the model into six faces. Organize and unfold these faces into a flat layout in the design tool.
  2. Generate Cutting Paths:
    • Top Face: Generate the cutting path for the top face using standard cutting parameters.
    • Vertical Faces: Adjust the parameters for the vertical faces, ensuring compression in the vertical direction and no significant deformation in the horizontal direction.
  3. Clamping and Calibration: Use a fixture to securely clamp the material, adjusting the clamping states as needed throughout the process.
  4. Cutting Process:
    • Top Face: Start by cutting the top face of the model.
    • Flip the Material: After cutting the top face, flip the material (like a book) and rotate it 90 degrees.
    • Back Face: Cut the back face using the generated cutting path.
    • Vertical Clamping: After cutting the top and back faces, clamp the material vertically for the remaining cuts.
    • Side Faces: Cut the four side faces in sequence using the adjusted parameters for vertical compression.
  5. Completion: After cutting all six faces, the model will be fully carved and ready for further processing or activation.
